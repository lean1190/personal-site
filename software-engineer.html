Communication For Software Engineers

What's the most important skill to develop as a software engineer?
Some say it's technical expertise and keeping up with trends.
I think the most important skill is communication.
Let me tell you why.

Why communication?

As software engineers, we spend a lot of our time communicating with our colleagues

  • We have dailies, retros, and other meetings where we talk to developers, product managers, designers, and other stakeholders
  • We write pull requests and code reviews
  • We do pair and mob programming
  • We are constantly making and explaining technical decisions, and discussing approaches
  • We produce written and visual documentation like API docs and architectural diagrams
  • We onboard and mentor team members
  • We share and receive feedback
  • We constantly name variables, functions, classes, methods, and tests
If we cannot get our message through, the quality of our work suffers.
I struggled a lot with this during the first years of my career. I used to overwrite my colleagues' commits without asking when I thought my approach was better in quality. As expected, not a lot of people wanted to work with me. I was so blindly convinced that I was "right", that I overlooked the harm I was causing.

I was lucky enough to have a wake-up call from one of my colleagues who pointed this out. I was shocked and sad that I caused that. On the bright side, that situation opened my eyes to everything else that happens in a software engineering team beyond writing code. I started asking for feedback and paying attention to the way I communicated and how it landed.
This increased my value as a team member, improved my perception of my colleagues, and helped me become a team lead of a software engineering team.
